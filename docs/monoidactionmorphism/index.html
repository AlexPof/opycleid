opycleid.monoidactionmorphism

Overview

In the framework of Opycleid, a category action is a faithful functor , where is a small category, and is the 2-category of finite sets and relations.

By definition, a morphism between two category actions and is a pair where

  • is a functor from to , and
  • is a lax natural transformation from to .

When and are monoids, the natural transformation is simply a morphism in (i.e. a relation) between the sets and ( and being the single objects of and ), such that for any morphism of the relation is included in the relation .

Currently, Opycleid only allows the definition of monoid action morphisms.

MonoidActionMorphism

opycleid.monoidactionmorphism.MonoidActionMorphism(monoidaction_source,monoid_action_dest,monoid_morphism,nat_trans_mapping)

Defines a monoid action morphism.

Arguments

  • monoidaction_source: an instance of opycleid.categoryaction.MonoidAction defining the source monoid action of the morphism.
  • monoidaction_dest: an instance of opycleid.categoryaction.MonoidAction defining the target monoid action of the morphism.
  • monoid_morphism: a dictionary defining the functor . The keys are operations (as strings) in monoidaction_source, the values are operations (as strings) in monoidaction_dest.
  • nat_trans_mapping: a dictionary defining the lax natural transformation . In the case of monoid actions, this is simply a relation between the image sets. The keys are elements of the object of monoidaction_source, the values are list of elements of the object of monoidaction_dest.
